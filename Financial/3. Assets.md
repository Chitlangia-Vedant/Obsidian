# Depreciation & Amortization

- Depreciation is a process of systematically allocation casts of long lived assets over the period during which the assets are expected to provide economic benefits.
$$Depreciation=\frac{Purchase Price-Salvage Value}{Life Of The Asset}$$
- Various method of depreciation, such as straight line, WDV and unit of production

- Owned land is never depreciated 
- Depreciation of intangible assets (patents, copyright) is called amortization
- Good will in an intangible asset
- For accounting purpose goodwill arises only in a purchase transaction
# Non-Recurring Items
- A company need to separate revenue & expenses into items that are likely to continue in the future and items that are not
- Helps analyst to predict future earning of the company
- Items that are not likely to continue can be classified as **Discontinued Operation**
	- An operation that the company has disposed in the current period or is planning to dispose in future
	- Discontinued operation are shown as a separate line, net of tax after net income from continuing operation
- Unusual or Infrequent item
	- These are either unusual in nature or infrequent in occurrence
	- Gain or loss from selling on equipment
	- They are shown as separate line item, but before tax and are included in the income from continuing operation
- Change in accounting policy
	- Refer to change from one accounting method to another. For example changing inventory valuation from LIFO & FIFO
	- It require retrospective application i.e. all prior period financial statement needs to be restated
	- Retrospective application, helps maintain comparability of the statement across period
- Changes in accounting estimate
	- this refer to change in the managements estimate. For example, changing the useful life of a depreciable assets
	- It require prospective application, i.e. no need to restate prior financial statement
- Correction of prior period error
	- This refer to an adjustment done to correct a prior period accounting error.
	- All prior period statement should be restate
	- In addition, disclosure of the nature of the adjustment is required in footnotes
# Non Operating Items
- Non Operation items are portion or expenses that relates to the activities not core to business operation.
- For a non financial services company, interest income & expenses are non operating in nature.
- For a financial services company, interest income & expenses are operating in nature.
