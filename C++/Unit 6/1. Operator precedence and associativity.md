
An **operation** is a mathematical process involving zero or more input values (called **operands**) that produces a new value (called an output value). The specific operation to be performed is denoted by a construct (typically a symbol or pair of symbols) called an **operator**.

# Operator precedence and associativity

Operators with a higher **precedence** level are grouped with operands first.

If two operators with the same precedence level are adjacent to each other in an expression, the operator’s **associativity** tells the compiler whether to evaluate the operators from left to right or from right to left.
