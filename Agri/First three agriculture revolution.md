The **[First Agricultural Revolution](https://study.com/academy/lesson/neolithic-agricultural-revolution-causes-and-implications.html)**, also known as the [Neolithic Revolution](https://study.com/learn/lesson/neolithic-revolution-history-food.html), began around 10,000 BCE. Humans shifted from being hunter-gathers to being subsistence farmers and herders. 
The **Second Agricultural Revolution**, or the British Agricultural Revolution, began around 300 years ago during the 18th century. Major changes to farming techniques included selectively breeding livestock, systematic crop rotation, and the development of chemical fertilizers. 
The **Third Agricultural Revolution**, or the [Green Revolution](https://study.com/academy/lesson/what-is-the-green-revolution-definition-benefits-and-issues.html), took place during the 1950s and 60s. Improvements in technologies for growing plants allowed for much greater [crop yields](https://study.com/academy/lesson/crop-yield-definition-consequences.html), helping to increase the world population.
The prioritization of high-yield crops, breeding, the use of fertilizers, livestock optimisation… All these factors led to an unprecedented surge in productivity.
 On the positive side, they enabled an ever growing population, and reduced the number of people needed to cultivate the food we need. On the negative side, both had a damaging impact on our environment and health
